I. State Government Supported Schemes/Programmes

Purpose

One Stop Centres (OSCs) are intended to provide integrated immediate, emergency and non-emergency support and assistance to women affected by violence, both in private and public spaces under one roof. Medical Assistance, Legal Support, Psychosocial Support, Police Assistance and short-term shelter at One Stop Centre and long term shelter at Swadhar Grehs are provided for the women affected by violence. 19 One Stop Centres are functional in 19 districts.

Admission Rules

The needy women along with their children (girls and boys upto 12 years) can avail temporary shelter for a maximum period of 5 days.

In case girls are referred, they will also be provided services in coordination with authorities/institutions established under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 and the Protection of Children from Sexual Offence Act, 2012.
The admissibility of any woman to the temporary shelter would be at the discretion of the Centre Administrator.
For long-term shelter requirements, arrangements will be made by OSCs in coordination with Shakti Sadan

Working Women Hostels

Working Women Hostels under WD&CW Dept provide food, shelter and security to young women employees of low-income group, who are away from families.

Services

Out of the total strength in the hostels, 20% to be provided for the students under training for employment for a period of two years with preference to departmental students of nurse training, women’s technical training institute and other employment training programmes

Target Group

  • Women who may be single, widowed, divorced, separated or married (whose immediate family does not reside in the same city/area).
  • Preference will be given to women from disadvantaged sections of the society.
  • Special preference would be given to beneficiaries with physical disabilities, in case there is shortage of accommodation.
  • Women who are under one year job training will also be considered for accommodation subject to availability.
  • The number of women under training for jobs should not exceed 30% of the total capacity.
  • Girls up to 18 years and boys up to the age of 12 years, accompanying working mothers will be provided accommodation with their mothers.
  • Working mothers may also avail of the services of the Day Care Centre, as provided under the scheme.

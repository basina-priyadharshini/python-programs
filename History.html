HISTORY OF KEDARNATH

Kedarnath Temple has a rich and ancient history deeply rooted in Hindu mythology and spiritual tradition. It is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva and holds immense religious significance for devotees across India.
According to legend from the Mahabharata, after the Kurukshetra war, the Pandavas sought forgiveness from Lord Shiva for the sins committed during the war. Lord Shiva avoided them and took the form of a bull (Nandi), hiding in the Garhwal Himalayas. When the Pandavas recognized him, Shiva dived into the ground. It is believed that different parts of his body appeared at different places‚ÄîKedarnath is where the hump of the bull emerged. These places together are known as the Panch Kedar.
Historically, the original temple is believed to have been built by the Pandavas, while the present stone structure was reconstructed and revived by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century CE. A statue of Adi Shankaracharya stands near the temple, and it is believed that he attained samadhi here.
The temple is built using massive stone slabs placed over a large rectangular platform, showcasing remarkable ancient engineering. Despite harsh weather conditions, earthquakes, and floods‚Äîmost notably the 2013 Uttarakhand floods‚Äîthe main temple structure remained largely intact, which many devotees consider a divine miracle.

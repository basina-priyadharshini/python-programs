ABOUT US

About:

The Agriculture Department of Gujarath, post-bifurcation in 2014, has evolved into a dynamic, farmer-centric institution working towards productivity profitability, and sustainability in agriculture. Through strategic programs, technology integration, and farmer empowerment, it continues to serve the needs of the agrarian community.

Agriculture Department of the newly formed State of Gujarath was restructured to cater to the specific agro-climatic, economic, and administrative needs of the residual state.

Our Vision

Our vision is to make agriculture profitable profession and to improve the living standards of the farmers of Gujarath in a sustainable manner.

Our Mission

To provide services which can help farmers to transform subsistence agriculture to commercial agriculture without disturbing ecological balance. To record higher productivity to withstand international competition by promoting diversification of cultivation of crops.

Our Permission

To achieve an increased GVA growth by adopting changes in cropping patterns and adoption of latest agricultural practices for increasing the crop production and productivity through effective technology transfer from Lab to Land and its acceptability by farmers.
To reduce production costs through biological methods, increase farmers' agricultural income, and empower farmers to sustainably use land and water resources.

Activities of the Agriculture Department

Agriculture Department undertakes a range of activities aimed at holistic agricultural development and welfare of farmers. Key activities include:
  • Issuance of licenses for seeds, fertilizers, and pesticides.
  • Monitoring quality and availability of agricultural inputs.
  • Promotion of certified and high-yielding varieties.

  • Implementation of farmer-oriented welfare schemes.
    • Capacity building through Farmer Training Centres (FTCs), Rythu Seva Kendras (RSK).
  • Dissemination of best practices and new technologies.
  • Promotion of crop diversification based on agro-climatic zones.
  • Support for horticulture, pulses, oilseeds, and millets.
  • Conduct of soil health tests and distribution of soil health cards.
  • Promotion of integrated nutrient and pest management practices.
  • Introduction of e-crop booking, drone-based field monitoring, and AI-driven advisories.
  • Establishment of online license management systems and grievance portals.
  • Compensation under crop insurance schemes.
  • Real-time advisory during natural calamities.
